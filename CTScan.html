160 Slice CT Scan

High-speed, low-dose imaging for precise diagnostics.

About 160 Slice CT Technology

A Computed Tomography (CT) scan combines a series of X-ray images taken from different angles around your body to create cross-sectional images (slices) of bones, blood vessels, and soft tissues. Our 160-slice CT scanner is a highly advanced system that captures numerous images at incredible speed. This allows for detailed 3D reconstructions of any body part, providing your doctor with a comprehensive view for a more accurate diagnosis, often with a lower radiation dose than older scanners.

Key Advantages of 160 Slice CT

  • check_circleRapid Scan Times: Reduces scan duration significantly, which is crucial for pediatric patients, trauma cases, and minimizing motion artifacts.
  • check_circleLower Radiation Dose: Advanced technology allows us to obtain high-quality images with optimized radiation levels for enhanced patient safety.
  • check_circleExceptional Image Detail: Provides crystal-clear images of even the smallest structures, essential for complex diagnoses like cardiac and vascular studies.
  • check_circleWide Range of Applications: Perfect for CT angiography, virtual colonoscopy, lung cancer screening, and detailed orthopedic assessments.

What to Expect During Your CT Scan

Our priority is your comfort and well-being throughout the procedure.

  1. Preparation: Depending on the type of scan, you may be asked to avoid eating or drinking for a few hours. You will need to remove metal objects, and in some cases, a contrast dye may be administered orally or intravenously to enhance the images.
  2. During the Scan: You will lie on a motorized table that moves through the center of the doughnut-shaped CT scanner. The scanner is open, so you won't feel enclosed. The technologist will guide you through the process, which is typically fast and painless.
  3. After the Scan: You can resume your normal activities immediately. If a contrast dye was used, you might be advised to drink plenty of fluids to help flush it from your system. Our radiologists will then interpret the results and prepare a report for your doctor.
